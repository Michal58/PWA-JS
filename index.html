Computer Graphics

This site is a compact guide to key concepts in computer graphics, illustrated through visual examples.

Terms Overview

  • Normal Vector – A perpendicular vector used in lighting calculations and surface orientation.
  • Triangle Geometry – Basic building blocks of 3D models composed of vertices and edges.
  • Perspective Projection – Technique for simulating depth by projecting 3D objects onto a 2D plane.
  • Shading – Methods for simulating light and shadow on surfaces for realistic rendering.
  • Ray Tracing – Rendering technique that simulates the path of light rays for high-quality visuals.
  • Pipelining – Graphics pipeline stages that process and render 3D scenes step-by-step.
  • Bézier Curve – Smooth curve defined by control points, used in vector graphics and animation.
  • Affine Transformation – Linear mappings like translation, rotation, and scaling applied to objects.
  • Interpolation – Estimating values (like color or position) between known data points.
